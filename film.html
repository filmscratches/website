Film Diary 🎞

A list of films I've watched in the cinema since I launched this page in March 2022, with the occassional streaming exclusive if it has a good reputation. Anything ★★★★ and above is an automatic recommendation.

I'm only going to see films that genuinely interest me. No content for content's sake (again, streaming), or deliberate hatewatching - life's too short for bad films. ✌

The Batman
dir: Matt Reeves
★★★ / watched March 18th
As a Kerrang kid, I remember each band hyping their new album as their darkest, loudest, angriest one yet. So Batman, portrayed excellently by Robert Pattinson, has gone full black makeup emo with no intention of keeping up the Bruce Wayne alter-ego. Good. The Riddler's wrapped his face in a bin bag and runs an evil Twitch stream. Great. Nirvana's Something in the Way plays constantly to emphasise this is all heavy shit. Fine. This, to beat the metaphor dead, is more technically proficient and structurally complex than anything else in the Batman oeuvre, but nobody will rank it as their favourite. Folks just want to bop around to Smells Like Teen Spirit.
tags: crime, mystery, thriller
Turning Red
dir: Domee Shi
★★★★ / watched March 12th
The initial annoyance of precocious Mei-Lee dissolves into a charming, often hilarious coming-of-age film that blends well a fantastical tribute to Asian culture with weird nostalgia for boy bands and Tamagotchis. This literal period piece — there's sanitary pads, people — feels substantial versus most of Pixar's output post-Toy Story 3, which makes its unceremonious dumping onto Disney+ a shame.
tags: animation, comedy, fantasy
Ali & Ava
dir: Clio Barnard
★★★ / watched March 7th
Ali and Ava's engaging burgeoning romance in a dystopian hellhole ("Bradford") is heightened by the woozy use of music as a means of escapism; making seeing this at the cinema worthwhile. It's certainly a British lottery funded film, though. Those Hollywood script doctors would never allow something so meandering and incident free through, insisting on at least one race against time to the airport. Sidenote: Ali & Ava contains the best damn production management ever committed to film (hi Rob!).
tags: drama, romance
